Otávio Manzano Kavakama, known in the Feitoria River region as the little scinax, began his musical studies at the age of seven, playing the recorder and the piano, with teacher Eliane, at the House of Culture Jandyra Massoni. At the house of José Ricardo, he dedicated himself to the practice of the guitar, accompanied by the esteemed Gabriel, today enjoying the cool breeze of the subtropical climate.

From the nylon strings to the distortion pedals, from Love Me Do to The Number of the Beast, from the mellow strum-strum to the deafening djent-djent, Toadinho developed jugular muscles and started to fall asleep to the sound of dying high frequencies. At the Pro Sound Music Institute, located in The Last City of the Metropolis’ The First City of the Countryside’s downtown, he goes from student to professor after a few years — path that included being an occasional dweller and moving-in helper. Worth to mention that it is during this period that the eighth son develops his only most important social skills , that are deteriorating at a fast pace.

While witnessing a concert of the written tradition of European “classical” music, it came to his attention the genesis of a newly formed Projeto Guri’s class. Wishing for some friction in his life, he soon develops an interest for the supposedly high-pitch descendant of the lira da braccio. However, after asking about the possibility of applying for such sonic artifact, he was asked back if he would not consider applying for an alternative artifact. The artifact in question was the little-big-progeny of the viola da braccio.

Indifferent regarding the different sizes, the aforementioned subject of this bio, named after the first Roman emperor, confirmed his application; this happening led to a felicitous eventuality: a relative, more specifically an aunt, more specifically named Eveline, had in her possession one of these air-exciting utensils that gives fruition to sonic waves. This aunt promptly lent him such apparatus.

From Guri to the Bowling Greens, many jumps were executed: teacher Luiza, Conservatory of Tatuí with teachers Sheila and Tiago, and private lessons with André, besides anthropomusical experiments. “I shall mention other teachers that I consider of vital importance to my growth as a person and musician,” he affirms, while resting by the upper river. “Fúlvio, Marcelo, Marcelo, Márcio, Ronaldo, and the entire 2012 Brazilian Women’s Volleyball team.”

By the end of the year just cited, he enjoyed a Spanish apple at the Mississippi river. There is, however, an appendix that is capital: there was a quick but exquisite experience inside USP’s walls; at Masp and Ibirapuera, he played his last notes on Tupi grounds before his departure to Northern lands.

In the birthplace of the Blues and Rock, he limped and thrived under the auspicious of Alexander, and for five year gathered a noble collection of synapses, not necessarily handy, but over here there is no marketing logic and pretension.

Northward he went, avoiding the aridity of the West, the eerie emptiness of the Northeast, and the algid cold of the far North — he was rejected in the East and had no commitments in the other cardinal points. And it is there that I find me here, ready for my fourth year, second focused on the present.

All the best,

Otávio

Bowling Green, OH, USA 08/20/2020
